I worked closely with Dr Clare Buckley (now at University of Manchester) to develop the Phytochrome system for zebrafish. The Phytochrome system is a light-induced heterodimerisation system that allows rapid, reversible and precise manipulation of proteins.

It had previously been used to dissect signalling pathways in single cells in culture. Clare optimised the system for the multicellular zebrafish embryo. As proof of principle, we moved the apical polarity protein Pard3 to different subcellular locations in various cell types. We were able to do this with high temporal and spatial precision within the living, normally developing zebrafish embryos. Importantly, when we recruited Pard3 to a particular subcellular location we also observed the accumulation of Pard6, suggesting that Pard3 still interacted with its normal binding partners during our manipulations.
